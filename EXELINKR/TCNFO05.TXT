  ³6. General information (the history of TC)
  ÀÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄÄ
     
   Our group  was found  in  March 1994 and called itself GERMAN DYNAMITE.  
   The theory behind  this name  was a symbiosis between small german demo-  
   groups which  cannot make  megademos  alone but have  talented  artists, 
   coders and musicians.  Due to the  fact that  some people  were thinking
   that  the  name  GERMAN DYNAMITE  would  have a fascism touch we renamed
   our group to THE COEXiSTENCE. Although that we are of course not facists
   nore we are focussed to that direction, we decided to rename our group.

   In April 1994 we released our first production, a BBS-advertisement  for
   our worldheadquarter FARPOiNT STATiON. This little program was a 7k addy
   with  nice  vectorgraphics and HSC-Music, which was coded by Jinx!.

   The next production was the TCP (THE COEXiSTENCE PLAYER). TCP is a module
   player which  can  play  huge modules  and  has  a  very  unusual  design 
   (referred to other module-player).  TCP was coded by InspirE,  our sound-
   specialist. Our player supports the Gravis Ultrasound.

   Well,  then there is UNEATABLE. It is our first megademo. We worked on it 
   about 2  months.  UNEATABLE  includes a  dancepart  with nice  music  and 
   amazing graphics.  
     
   The next release was XLINK. XLINK allows you to link *.exe and
   data files to one big exe. Read the docs for more information about it.
   There will be updates for XLINK soon.

   'Contagion', our contribution to THE PARTY IV, won the 3rd place at the
   international democompetition there.

   Introducing our forthcoming musicdisk (which will be released soon),
   we released a rave module made by our composer DUKE. 

   The latest releases are two musicfiles by our musician Libertine.

   Our plans for the future are the making of more demonstrations, intros
   and even commercial products and games etc.
   If you have any questions or other things you would like to discuss with
   us, do not hesitate to contact us (Please refer to the "contact"-chapter
   of this textfile).
